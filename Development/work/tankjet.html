Tank Jet Studios

Coming off a freelancing stint of almost 5 years, I had gotten to a fork where my work was suffering from lack of perspective. There were two other guys in a similar position that I knew from friends of friends. We got together and formed Tank Jet Studios. We did some great work for some large brands, we had a blast, failed, and most importantly learned how to grind, and build a brand.

1. Creating our brand by crafting a story

We were competing for work from RFPs and no one knew us. We needed our brand to not only stick out, but pique interest to be memorable. We wrote a story and created the brand assets from that. Our brand assets were more popular globally than our work ever was.

3. Our Products

Though client work paid the bills we all longed for solving problems. So after about 6 months of growth we ventured out on our own with three products.

Domesticated

This product was the brainchild of Tank Jet Partner Matt. He's a really talented developer and designer so he created most of the initial concepts. I wasn't involved until we began building the web app and creating awareness. We since took it down but we collected a large database of Made-in-USA products. Relationships were estabished with founders of companies and we learned a lot about what's always been refered to as American Spirit.

Mine Games

After Domesticated we decided it would be fun to build a game. We did a bunch of research, which included a lot of game nights. We collaborated on concepting, which was centered around making a game that would appeal to a wide age range. Mine Games is a digital deck-building game (similar to Magic or Dominion) that allows teams up to 3 to play with each other. Jeremy did the illustration, Matt did the back-end, and I did the interface and front-end development.
We built the app using Phonegap, which I've since fell in love with for prototyping.
