Smart Loan

Working at Capital One Labs was unique in that you got so much exposure to different types of design. This project was a cross between service design, financial instrument feature design, and digital UX. The output was an alternative for exploitive payday loans. The loan has accompanying mechanisms to provide easy access to formal banking instruments so that the likelihood of repeat usages was greatly declined.

1. Research

We knew there was a reason why customers left us as a company after a pretty uniform amount of time, we just weren’t sure what it was. We set off to learn more about the people that make up the subprime segment and what makes them tick.

a. Learning Agenda

We needed to create an outline of the things we needed to learn. None of the people on the team had any personal experience with pay day loans. The first step was learning all we can about exploitive lending practices.

b. Experiment Design

I designed an experiment using card sorting to dig into how the user makes a decision about who to borrow from.

c. Empathy Interviews

My team and I performed 8 empathy interivews to learn about why people end up using Payday Loans and what causes the need. We learned that the causes range immensely but typically center around things that make getting to work impossible.

Another main learning is that people typically take out another loan to cover the principal of the first, creating a cycle that can reach +1000% APR.

“"The way these products are structured, it's very difficult to repay the loan. People end up borrowing again and again and paying far more in fees and interest than they borrowed in the first place.” - Interviewee

2. Synthesis and Ideation

a. Experience Mapping

Experience Maps are probably one of my favorite tools in my UX toolbox. I use it with every project I take on, and this was no different. Filling out the spaces let me know what are the real pain points and whats important to design for.

>

b. Ideation

We had a team of 2 Product Managers, a Business Analyst, and myself. Ideation can tend to lean towards being single sighted, so its important to bring in other perspectives. We held an “ideation tour” in which we held multiple rounds in different offices with different kinds of employees.

c. Concepts

I spent about 2-weeks heads down, creating low fidelity concepts of the top ideas we collected throughout ideation. Click the play button below to view some of them.

d. User Feedback

We then did another round of Empathy Interviews that focused on understanding which concepts resonated with our user base.

3. Interface Design

After we had the feedback from our user base I felt confident in creating a user experience that could make it easy to apply anywhere (say at a repair shop, on your phone) and quick to get a decision. I built out an experience that was responsive, and touch capable.

4. Design Guideline Design

I was told I was going to be moving to a new project. To ensure that the next designer had the information necessary to design for the user I created a small style guide. The guide explains the whys behind some of the design decisions I've made.
